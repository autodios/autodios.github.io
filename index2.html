Paola Torres Núñez del Prado

Paola Torres Núñez del Prado (PE) explores the boundaries and connections in between tactility, audio and visual, the human voice, the sounds of nature (water, wind) and those whose listening is often considered less harmonious, such as machine sounds, noise, or digitally generated sounds. Her work is essentially complex: she explores the limits of the senses, examining the concepts of interpretation, translation and misrepresentation, so to analyze the construction of a cultural hegemony in relation to the history of technology and the arts.

She was awarded an Honorary Mention in Prix Ars Electronica in 2021, received a Google Artists + Machine Intelligence Residence Grant in 2019 and Vivo Arte.mov Mobile Media Award, Brasil, in 2013. She has presented her work and performances in various countries of the Americas, Central Europe, and Scandinavia, where she is based, which is also part of the collections of Malmo Art Museum and the Public Art Agency of Sweden.
